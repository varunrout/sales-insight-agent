# Market Overview

## Regional outlook

North America is the core commercial market and should carry the highest confidence forecast assumptions. Demand is more stable than other regions, the Direct channel is mature, and enterprise customers support higher average order values.

EMEA offers meaningful revenue, but execution risk rises in Q3. The risk is concentrated in Partner-sourced demand, where seasonal buying pauses, slower partner follow-up and additional discounting can reduce conversion. Management should treat EMEA Q3 as a known planning risk rather than a surprise variance.

APAC is the fastest-growing region. Growth is supported by campaign response, rising new customer volume and product adoption in developing commercial segments. Pricing pressure is the main risk because regional growth is not yet matched by North American margin levels.

LATAM remains opportunistic. It can benefit from campaign-led demand and local market momentum, but volatility is high. Plans should include wider confidence ranges for LATAM revenue and order count.

## Pricing and promotion risks

Promotion periods create clear uplift, especially for Marketplace. This supports acquisition and short-cycle demand generation, but it can also train buyers to wait for discounts. Discount governance should separate strategic acquisition offers from margin-eroding broad promotions.

Enterprise products should be protected from unnecessary promotion depth. Their high average order value means a small number of deals can move revenue materially, but deep discounts can weaken gross margin and reduce perceived premium value.

## Channel risks

Direct is the preferred channel for margin quality and predictable execution. Partner remains valuable for scale, but it requires stronger enablement, pipeline hygiene and Q3 readiness in EMEA. Marketplace is useful for campaigns and long-tail demand, but it is promotion-sensitive and should not be used as the primary route for premium enterprise offers.

## Planning implications

The regional plan should combine stable North American assumptions, specific EMEA Partner mitigation actions, continued APAC growth investment and conservative LATAM volatility buffers. Channel mix matters as much as regional mix: shifting more revenue through Direct improves margin, while over-reliance on Marketplace can lift units without improving profit quality.
